Wilkinson's Data Transformations and their Uses:
An Exploration through Real-World Examples

In Table 4.1 of Leland Wilkinsonâ€™s Grammar of Graphics, Wilkinson defines three classes of data variable transformations: mathematical, statistical, and multivariate. Although he provides a thorough list and brief explanations of these twenty-eight data transformations, Wilkinson does not provide real or synthetic examples of when and how these data transformations are applied. Furthermore, Wilkinson does not provide context for the value of these transformations in the creation of meaningful data visualizations. For this reason, we have selected a collection of these twenty-eight data transformations to study various real world data sets. In these studies, we seek to illuminate use cases of these transformations as they relate to data visualization and explore their intrinsic value in the field.
