Polarization in the American Electorate: Changing Opinions Regarding High-Profile Interest Groups

Figure 3: This time-series line graph describes how respondents feel about interest groups on a scale of 1 to 94. We plotted the changing standard deviation of each question over time, which we use to identify how controversial a certain group is within the American electorate. Greater standard deviations indicate greater dissonance of opinion regarding interest groups in the American electorate. Lower standard deviation indicates more unified opinions regarding interest groups. Please note: this does not necessarily indicate positive views on interest groups.
